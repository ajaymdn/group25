Introduction

This website describes the work of group 25 on the task of Adjustment of interactions and gestures with children's gamesâ€‹ within MotionInput v3.2 as part of the UCL System Engineering course. We aim to provide our research with all the relevant materials here, creating a single place where to store all our progress.

The MotionInput project seeks to pioneer touchless computing technology developed by UCL Computer Science students in collaboration with leading industry partners. Our team is adapting MotionInput software for the game Sonic Frontiers in the hope of showcasing the impressive potential of motion-controlled games for both mainstream gamers and people with disabilities.

Here below is a video of the first prototype of UCL Motion Input v3.1 which shows gameplay for the Sonic the Hedgehog game series (Sonic Frontiers and Sonic the Hedgehog 2) using Motion Input. With Sonic Frontiers we were able to implement:

  • Movement (Forward, Left and Right, Backwards) - This is implemented for both standing and sitting
  • Jump
  • Dashing to a target

As for Sonic the Hedgehog 2, we were able to implement:

  • Movement
  • Jump
