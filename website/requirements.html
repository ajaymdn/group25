Project Background

UCL MotionInput (UCLMI) is a software package that is initially developed as a response to the COVID-19 pandemic. The concept of MotionInput was started as a final year project in September 2020, with the first version being released in Jan 2021, this was developed by 2 final year BSc Computer Science students.

The first version (MotionInput V1) had support for 2 modes of interaction: The initial being a “Desk mode”, which enabled the user to replace their mouse control with holding a pen in the air. The second being “Exercise mode”, which allowed the user the interact via exercises such as walking on the spot.

To proceed with this concept of MotionInput, the project was taken over by a group of MSc Computer Science students - finally leading to the release of MotionInput V2. This was redeveloped and expanded to enable functionality to 4 different types of motion, including: Hands, Full Body, Eyes and Head tracking.

Recently the release of MotionInput V3 is underway, their being a release to the Microsoft Store, as everyone proceeds to make updates and work on MotionInput v3.2.
